# Snowflake customer data theft campaign — 2024

The Snowflake incident compromised **Confidentiality**, because attackers accessed and stole large volumes of data from Snowflake customer environments, including organizations 
such as AT&T and Ticketmaster/Live Nation. The group responsible for the attack is known as UNC5537, also referred to as ShinyHunters. The attackers exploited stolen credentials obtained through infostealer malware. Many customer environments lacked multi-factor authentication (MFA), allowing unauthorized access using just usernames and passwords.

# CDK Global ransomware attack — June 2024

The CDK Global attack compromised **Availability**, because CDK took major dealer management systems offline, disrupting operations of North American auto dealerships. 
It caused significant disruptions for over 15,000 auto dealerships across North America. The attack resulted in an estimated $1 billion in losses and forced many dealerships to revert to manual operations for nearly two weeks. The threat actor was a cybercriminal ransomware group called BlackSuit, which has links to the Royal/Conti ransomware ecosystem.

# XZ Utils backdoor — March 2024

The XZ Utils incident compromised **Integrity**, because malicious code was deliberately inserted into a trusted open-source compression library used by Linux systems. It also threatened Confidentiality and Availability, because the backdoor could have allowed unauthorized remote access to affected systems through SSH, potentially leading to data theft or system disruption. The backdoor was inserted by a user named "Jia Tan" over a two-year campaign to gain maintainer access. The XZ Utils backdoor raised serious concerns about the security of open-source software. It highlighted vulnerabilities in the software supply chain and the potential for sophisticated attacks that exploit the development process itself.
